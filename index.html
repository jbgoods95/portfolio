Background

Growth marketing showed me where things were breaking. Product marketing is how I fixed them.

At Keeping Current Matters (KCM), I spent seven years running the growth function, managing spend, optimizing funnels, measuring what moved. But the work that truly moved the business was never the tactics downstream. It was the strategic thinking upstream: understanding why customers were not converting, what they needed to believe before they would act, and how the product and its positioning needed to change together to address that.

For most of those seven years, that product marketing work was my primary focus: studying the market, shaping the messaging, building the landing pages, and feeding customer insight back to the product team to inform what got built next. That work drove ARR from $3.5M to $18M. Growth marketing was how we scaled it. Product marketing is what made it possible.

I work at the intersection of customer insight, product strategy, and go-to-market execution, turning confusion into clarity and aligning teams around a narrative that guides both what gets built and how it reaches the market. My growth background gives me a perspective most PMMs never develop: I have seen what happens downstream when positioning is wrong. That is what drives me to get it right upstream.

RealTalk: From Insight to $18M ARR

Full GTM ownership for a premium video product — customer insight reshaping product direction, a feature-to-transformation positioning shift, a successful launch, and three years of sustained growth.

Customer Research Positioning Strategy GTM Design Messaging Architecture AI Feature Launch Sustained Growth
+

The Problem

Keeping Current Matters had scaled quickly post-COVID, then hit a growth plateau. The instinct was to optimize channels: more spend, new creative, better targeting. When I dug into the data and customer feedback, the real issue was different. A video product was in development, but adoption of the existing video offering was weak. The question was why.

Through customer feedback, behavioral data, and sales conversations, a clear pattern emerged: agents knew video was important for their business, but they felt uncertain on camera, did not know what to say, and did not have time to figure it out.

"The key insight: agents weren't failing to use video because the product was broken. They were failing because they didn't believe they could do it. Feature improvements alone wouldn't fix that: the positioning and the product experience needed to change together."

The Positioning Decision

The highest-leverage move in the entire launch was the positioning shift: from feature-focused to transformation-focused, from what the product does to who you become.

Before

"The first all-in-one video creator for agents" — features led, tool positioning. Objection: agents don't need another tool.

After

"Show up as the trusted expert, making video simple, fast, and confident" — transformation led, identity positioning. Insight: agents need permission and confidence.

I brought this confidence and clarity insight to product and leadership to reshape not just the positioning but the product roadmap. The teleprompter with expert-written scripts directly addressed "I don't know what to say." Rehearsal mode addressed camera anxiety. One-tap sharing addressed time and technical barriers. AI-powered script personalization addressed authenticity concerns. The product evolved because the insight demanded it.

Launch Strategy

The launch was sequenced across member and non-member segments, with each phase designed to maximize intent before opening to cold audiences.

Member Pre-Launch

Exclusive early access for existing KCM members with a lifetime discount to drive urgency and reward loyalty.

Non-Member Hotlist

Video e-guide lead magnet and hotlist pre-registration to build a warm, intent-qualified audience before launch day.

Official Launch

4-part launch sequence across email, paid, organic, partners, and webinar with multiple CTAs mapped to funnel stage.

Post-Launch Momentum

Masterclass lead magnet to webinar to trial offer sequence, extending launch momentum into the post-launch window.

Sustained Growth: Three Years of Iteration

RealTalk was not a single launch event. I owned the product marketing continuously for three years, through multiple feature releases and ongoing experimentation. A weekly testing framework across ads, landing pages, and lifecycle kept performance compounding. CTR improved from approximately 1% to 3%+. The landing page redesign, which was a messaging rewrite from feature-heavy to outcome-focused, increased trial form clicks by 50% and enabled ad spend to scale from 80K to 60K per month without sacrificing efficiency.

Feature launches during this period included the ScriptBuilder 2.0 AI feature (positioned as "AI in your voice" to address authenticity concerns agents had with generic AI tools) and a Recording Studio expansion that repositioned the product from mobile-only to desktop and mobile, removing friction for a significant segment of agents.

Playbooks: Turning a Database Problem into a Product

New product GTM for a recurring outreach system, positioning a "show, not tell" product, designing the launch and onboarding experience, and achieving the highest registrant-to-trial conversion in two years.

New Product GTM Positioning Onboarding Design In-App Messaging Launch Execution
+

The Problem

Real estate agents statistically close only 8% of the deals that happen within their sphere of influence. The rest go to competitors, not because the agent was less capable, but because they failed to stay present with their database through meaningful, timely outreach. CRMs and lead-gen tools helped agents collect contacts. They did not help agents know what to say, when to say it, or how to say it in a way that felt human rather than automated.

"Agents didn't have a lead problem. They had a conversation problem. The opportunity was to build something that eliminated the guesswork from outreach, not by automating it, but by making it structured, repeatable, and grounded in what was actually happening in the market."

Positioning the Product

Playbooks was harder to position than RealTalk. RealTalk solved a visible, felt problem: agents knew they needed video. Playbooks solved a problem agents had largely normalized: their database was inactive, and they had accepted that as the cost of doing business.

The positioning had to make the problem feel urgent and solvable without leaning on fear or guilt. Testing during the launch confirmed that pain-based messaging significantly underperformed. Confidence and results-based messaging, showing agents the transactions silently happening in their database without them, drove stronger engagement across ads and email alike.

The core positioning: Conversations to Deals. Not a tool, not generic content. A system that gives agents everything they need to activate their database through high-impact, market-driven conversations, and then guides them through doing it step by step.

GTM and Launch Execution

The launch was anchored by a live workshop, a format chosen specifically because Playbooks required demonstration, not just description. The workshop let agents experience the product's value in real time before committing to a trial.

I owned the full launch motion: lead magnet design (a free downloadable Playbook to build the warm list), webinar strategy and execution, launch email sequencing, paid social campaign strategy, and the trial onboarding experience. The onboarding flow was designed around a single value moment: getting a new member to successfully share their first piece of content within their first session, with every prompt and in-app message written to accelerate that outcome and reduce time to activation.

Chroma: Positioning a New Product Category from Zero

0-to-1 positioning and GTM design for a new subscription membership: ICP development, narrative architecture, category design, and a live event launch for a product that did not yet exist in the market.

ICP Development Positioning Architecture Category Design GTM Strategy Offer Design
+

Context

Streamline is a national art education company whose business had been built on high-ticket live and virtual events. Events generated enthusiasm and revenue, but created a structural gap: artists attended, got inspired, and then had no structured path to keep growing. Revenue was event-dependent. Retention between events was weak.

The opportunity was to build a subscription membership that would convert event energy into ongoing, structured growth. This required defining what the product actually was, who it was for, why it was different from every other online art education on the market, and how to introduce it to an audience that had never seen it before.

"The market was full of art content libraries, workshops, and online courses. Chroma had to stand for something different. The insight: artists weren't failing because they lacked content or talent. They were failing because they lacked a clear path and consistent structure. The enemy wasn't other products: it was guesswork."

ICP Development

I built a comprehensive audience profile using survey data, behavioral analysis, and customer interview synthesis — targeting adults aged 50–75, semi-retired or retired, college-educated, ranging from affluent hobbyists to semi-professionals.

Beyond demographics, I mapped the emotional and motivational layer using Steven Reiss's 16 Basic Desires framework and Bain's Elements of Value. The primary desires were acceptance, status, curiosity, social contact, and tranquility, which directly shaped messaging tone and the emphasis on community belonging. The core desire: to build skill and confidence, to feel proud of progress and inspired by potential, without pressure, comparison, or rigid rules.

This research directly shaped both product architecture and messaging. Each of the six product pillars: Clear Path, Structured Practice, Expert Guidance, Community Support, Accountability, and Recognition, each mapped to a specific barrier uncovered in the ICP work.

Positioning Architecture

The Market

Content libraries, one-off workshops, and online courses. Audiences overwhelmed by too many options, no clear direction, inconsistent progress.

Chroma's Position

"Not another content library. Not another one-off event. Not another art school. A guided path for artists who are ready to stop guessing and start growing."

The full positioning framework moved through problem definition (artists stuck in guesswork), naming the enemy (guesswork itself, not other products or talent), the solution frame (six elements that work together for real progress), the differentiator (guided structure plus community accountability), and the brand promise: Chroma — Where Artists Grow Together.

GTM: Founding Class at PACE

The launch was designed around Streamline's flagship PACE event in May 2026: a four-day live event attended by thousands of the exact audience Chroma was built for. Rather than launching cold, PACE gave us a captive, warm audience who already trusted Streamline and had just experienced the energy that Chroma was designed to sustain.

Pre-Event Priming

Email sequence building anticipation before arrival: "something new is coming" without revealing the product.

Day 1: Awareness

Faculty introduces BrushUp Method concepts organically in sessions. Handouts distributed. Founder hints at announcement.

Day 2: Reveal

Chroma reveal video. Founding Class invitation presented live on stage. QR code enrollment opens. Info table activated.

Days 3–4: Urgency

Faculty demos reinforce the Chroma connection. Founding Class progress updates. Bonuses expire at end of PACE. Post-event email sequence begins.

Cross-functional alignment required briefing faculty artists on how to introduce BrushUp Method concepts naturally during sessions, aligning the product owner on offer structure and pricing logic, preparing staff briefing documents for info desks and VIP tables, and coordinating all brand and visual assets including the logo, landing page, slide deck, and printed handouts.

Offer Design

The Founding Class offer was designed to balance urgency, exclusivity, and long-term retention. Monthly at $59/month versus Annual at $499/year (30% off, price locked for three years). Limited to 200 Founding Members: scarcity tied to a genuine product constraint around cohort quality, not artificial urgency. Founding benefits reinforced identity rather than just savings: a Founding Member badge, name on the Founding Class page, and a private session with the founder.

The guarantee was framed around effort rather than outcomes: "If you do the work and don't feel progress, we refund you." This signals confidence in the product while shifting the risk frame in a way that resonates with an audience motivated by personal growth, not transactional value.

Note: Chroma launches at PACE in May 2026. This case study documents the positioning and GTM strategy work completed in advance of launch. Results will be updated post-launch.
